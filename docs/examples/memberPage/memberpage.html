Brian Crooks

ARTIST/WRITER/FAN/DRAWER

Growing up, Brian Crooks lived in four states before his tenth birthday. He says that during all of those moves, he spent a lot of time reading comic books and telling himself stories that would become the basis for his work. "My favorite was always Nightcrawler," he says. "I've always loved that character design and thought his backstory was really interesting. I'd really like to develop a character that iconic someday." As both writer and illustrator, Brian prides himself on world building and creating a fully fleshed out environment in which his characters live. "To me, there's nothing more frustrating than a book with great-looking characters and quality writing, but a world that doesn't make sense or rules that feel arbitrary," he adds.

His current project, War & Money, is a perfect example of how that time and preparation lead to a world that leaves the reader wanting more. "I started working on this concept about twelve or thirteen years ago," he explains. "It just started with the concept of war as a pre-scheduled kind of spectator event. I remember thinking up this scene of a guy sitting down with his coffee and the newspaper, reading the headline, 'War Breaks Out At 2 PM.' From there, the whole thing just grew organically." Over the years, that nugget of an idea grew into the full book. "I like to think about it as a world where the top spectator sport is war. Not the kind of war where thousands of people die and planes are dropping bombs on cities and everything, but war transformed into a series of hand-to-hand fights with distinct styles and disciplines. When you strip out the stakes of innocent life and huge costs of maintaining a standing army of tens of thousands of soldiers, you really open up a lot of possibilities for the way countries interact with one another, and the way to public interprets the entire concept of war." Crooks also took a lot of inspiration from one of his other favorite pastimes, professional and collegiate sports. "When I thought about fleshing out this world, a lot of it came from the different environments you see at big sporting events," he explains. "The difference between a football Saturday at the University of Michigan and a World Cup match between Croatia and Iceland served as great inspiration for how each of these individual countries handle the spectacle of war as performative exercise." See what Brian created in his debut graphic novel, War & Money, and stay tuned for more from this up-and-coming writer and illustrator.
